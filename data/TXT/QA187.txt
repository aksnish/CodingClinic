Question: How would a diagnosis of “postoperative seroma” be coded in ICD-10-CM? 
Answer: Currently, ICD-10-CM’s Index to diseases, directs the  coder  to see  “hematoma” when  the  term “seroma”  is  referenced.  The  specific  code assignment for postoperative seroma would depend on the body system involved in the surgery. For example, codes in subcategory I97.6-, Postprocedural hemorrhage and hematoma  of a circulatory system organ or structure following a procedure,  are assigned based on  whether the seroma occurred following a cardiac bypass, cardiac catheterization or other circulatory system procedure.
